About Me

Specifically, I am studying the largest dwarf satellite galaxies of the Milky Way (MW), the Large and Small Magellanic Clouds (LMC and SMC), trying to uncover their past evolutionary histories.

Current Research

My current research entails using photometric data taken with the MOSAIC II camera on the Cerro Tololo Inter-American Observatory (CTIO) 4-meter Blanco telescope using the Washington M , T2 and the DDO51 filter system. The goal is to target red clump (RC) and red giant branch (RGB) stars in the outer periphery of the LMC. Additionally, I use spectroscopic data of giant star candidates with the CTIO 4-meter HYDRA multi-fiber spectrograph and the MIKE spectrograph on the Magellan-2 Clay 6.5 m telescope at Las Campanas Observatory. This data is apart of an expansion of the MAgellanic Periphery Survey (MAPS; Nidever et al. 2011; Majewski et al. 2009) a more comprehensive effort to map the stellar periphery of both the Large and the Small Magellanic Clouds. MAPS combines photometry and spectroscopic follow-up of giant star candidates with the goal of investigating the interaction history of the Magellanic Clouds (MCs) by studying the structural and kinematical properties of their periphery populations.
