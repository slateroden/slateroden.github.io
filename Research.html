More on my Current Research: LMC Periphery Analysis

A long outstanding question over dwarf satellite galaxies (like the LMC) is whether or not they host stellar halos, begging the question of wether or not possesion of a stellar halo is dependent on disk size in spiral galaxies. Therefore, to help answer this question I am currently using photometric ans spectroscopic data searching for the outer edge of the LMC, possibly belonging to a bound stellar halo or stripped tidal debris from interactions between the MW and SMC. This is accomplished by using Washington M, T2 and DD051 filter photometry (Majewski et al. 2000) to differentiate red clump (RC) and red giant branch (RGB) stars from dwarf foregorund stars. Additionally, I am using spectroscopic data from the CTIO 4-meter HYDRA multi-fiber spectrograph and the MIKE spectrograph on the Magellan-2 Clay 6.5 m telescope to obtain radial velocity (RV) information of these giant stars. Thanks the Gaia data release 3 (DR3), we now have accurate proper motions (PMs) that I have used in conjuction with the RVs to probabiliticly select a group of likley-LMC giant stars to belong to the LMC out to as far as ~ 29 kpc. The results of this analysis is forthcoming in a soon to be published paper...
